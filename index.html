Coffee in Japan

Japan's best coffee shops import beans from coffee-growing countries around the world, such as Guatemala, Rwanda, Kenya, Indonesia and Thailand. Beans tend to be roasted and ground on site in small batches. Aeropress, French press, and paper drip (pour over) are common preparation methods.

Beaches in Japan

In the summer, beaches along the Shonan Coast and Miura Peninsula in Kanagawa Prefecture are very popular. The Izu Peninsula is often considered to have the best beaches and snorkeling near Tokyo, while the Boso Peninsula is particularly popular amongst surfers. The Izu Island chain south of Tokyo, including Izu Oshima, offers more beaches.

Valleys

Jigokudani or "Hell Valley" is a spectacular, appropriately named valley
just above the town of Noboribetsu Onsen, which displays hot steam vents, sulfurous streams
and other volcanic activity. It is a main source of Noboribetsu's hot spring waters.

Hanami

From the end of March to April in Japan, the cherry blossoms bloom and people go flower viewing (hanami) under the trees. A typical hanami is when you watch the flowers while eating and drinking together with your friends.


Mt. Koyasan

Mount Koya is the center of Shingon Buddhism, an important Buddhist sect which was introduced to Japan in 805 by Kobo Daishi (also known as Kukai), one of Japan's most significant religious figures.

Visit Disneyland!

Tokyo Disneyland is a theme park based on the films produced by Walt Disney. It was opened in 1983 as the first Disney theme park outside of the United States. Modeled after Disneyland in California and the Magic Kingdom in Florida, Tokyo Disneyland is made up of seven themed lands and features seasonal decorations and parades.
